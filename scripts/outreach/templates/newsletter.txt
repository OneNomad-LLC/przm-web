przm.sh shipped v0.1 today: a vendor-neutral, deterministic, Ed25519-
signed benchmark suite for AI reliability failure modes. The headline

Specifics of what's on the leaderboard right now:
- 30 hand-curated fixtures across 5 categories, every correct answer
  verified against authoritative sources before commit.
- 6 adapter configurations: baseline-Anthropic Haiku 4.5
  (synchronous and sequential reveal), baseline-Azure on gpt-5-mini
  and gpt-4o-mini (synchronous and sequential reveal), and AutoGen
  RoundRobinGroupChat on gpt-4o-mini.
- 12 Ed25519-signed receipts (each adapter on combined-30 + sealed
  6-fixture holdout) with full per-round transcripts.
- Pure-function scoring. No LLM in the grading loop.
- Methodology cites Peacemaker or Troublemaker (arXiv 2509.23055)
  and Disentangling Drivers of LLM Social Conformity (arXiv 2508.14918).

The most striking signed-receipt finding, held constant on
gpt-4o-mini: on the sealed 6-fixture holdout, AutoGen collapsed 0 of
6 scenarios; the hand-rolled baseline collapsed 5 of 6. On the
30-fixture combined run: AutoGen 10%, sync baseline 73%, sequential-
reveal baseline 87%. The gap holds after controlling for reveal
protocol, so the framework is doing real work beyond just letting
agents see each other within a round. This goes unmeasured because
the eval companies positioned to measure it sell to the same AI app
builders whose frameworks would be benchmarked.
