# przm Vendor Certification

## What it is

A signed, third-party receipt of your model or framework's score on the przm benchmark suite.

It's not a certificate with a logo on it. It's a cryptographically-signed JSON document that pins your adapter version, the exact LLM you ran, the fixture SHA-256, and your scores on every axis — generated by us, independent of you, using infrastructure you didn't control.

You can publish it on your website. You can link it from your README. You can put it in a sales deck. It's a performance attestation from a vendor-neutral third party that anyone can verify against our public key.

---

## Why buy it

Because "our agents are reliable" is something every framework vendor says. A przm receipt is the thing that makes it verifiable.

The benchmark harness is Apache-2.0 and anyone can run it. The difference between running it yourself and buying certification: when *you* run the bench and publish the number, your prospective customers know you saw the fixtures first. When we run it, the score is independent. That independence is the product.

If your score is good, you get marketing that's harder to argue with than a blog post.

If your score isn't good, you find out before your users do — and you get a signed record of exactly which scenarios caused it, which tells your engineering team exactly where to improve.

## What it does NOT include

**You do not see the fixtures in advance.** That's the point. If you saw the test questions, the score would be meaningless. The holdout set is sealed from everyone vendor-side, including us — it's never read during adapter development.

**We do not tune the benchmark to your strengths.** The scenarios are designed to break things, not to flatter them. If your framework has a sycophancy problem, the receipt will say so.

**We do not offer retakes before publishing.** A certification run is a certification run. If you want to improve your score, ship a better version and certify that release. Historical receipts stay in the ledger with their original scores.

**Certification is not an endorsement.** A przm receipt says "this is what we found when we ran the test." It does not say "przm recommends this product." A vendor with a 0.78 `correct_final_answer_rate` can certify, and so can a vendor with a 0.43. Both scores are defensible to publish — the meaning depends on context and the comparison leaderboard.

## How the process works

1. **Email us:** [hello@onenomad.dev](mailto:hello@onenomad.dev?subject=przm%20vendor%20certification) with your framework name, the version you want certified, and which LLM model your framework will run.

2. **You provide a sample API key** for the LLM endpoint your adapter uses — enough to run the full fixture set (estimate: ~$5–15 in API costs at current model pricing). We use your key so the run is billed to you, not us, and you can independently audit what was called.

3. **We implement or receive your adapter.** If you already have a pull request against the przm-bench repo with your adapter, we use that. If not, we implement a baseline adapter against your API and you review it before we run.

4. **We run the bench.** Temperature 0, seeded where the framework supports it. For frameworks that don't support deterministic seeds, we run each scenario three times and take the median per-axis score — this is documented in the receipt.

5. **We send you the private findings brief.** You have 48 hours to flag any adapter-implementation errors (wrong confederate injection, parser bug, etc.) before the receipt is signed. We will fix genuine implementation bugs. We will not rerun because the score was low.

6. **We sign and publish the receipt.** Standard delivery within 5 business days. Extended/Enterprise within 72 hours.

## Questions

**Can I certify a model instead of a framework?**
Yes. LLM providers can certify a specific model version against the baseline adapter. Contact us for model-specific pricing.

**What if I disagree with the methodology?**
The methodology is open source. Submit a PR. We take adversarial feedback seriously — it's in the methodology spec that competitors can submit replacement confederate prompts and we'll publish both runs. If your objection is substantive, it makes the benchmark better.

**What if the benchmark changes between my v1 cert and my v2 cert?**
Receipts pin the benchmark version. A `convergence-v0.1` receipt is always comparable to other `convergence-v0.1` receipts. When the benchmark version bumps, prior receipts stay in the ledger with their version label.
